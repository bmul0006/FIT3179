Crop Production Around the Globe

A visualisation by Brandon Alexander

Despite the fact that some countries have large land sizes, not all of their land are arable/farmable. USA is the country with the highest arable land of 1,577,368 km2 in the world but is approximately only 16.8% of the total land.

In terms of crop production per square km of arable land, Malaysia sits at the top with a production of 238,057,699 despite having a small arable land size of 8260 square km.

Predictably, countries with larger farmable land tend to have higher crop yield and harvest area with China, India, USA and Brazil being the top countries.

Countries with a small land size such as Singapore and Iceland are unable to produce crops in large volumes.

CLICK the selection bar on the bottom to find out the top 5 crops for each country in 2019!

Despite environmental changes and urbanisation, crop production all over the world has continued to grow over the years.

That being said, not all countries experienced the same positive growth as India has managed to increase their crop production from 1.6 billion to 2.4 billion in less in 18 years whereas a country like Australia has fluctuated between 100 to 300 million.
